About the Portal

Why This System Exists

University rankings don't evaluate themselves. Behind every QS or THE score is a year of data collection — hundreds of answers, documents, URLs, and evidence parcels gathered from across 12 departments. Without a coordinated system, critical data gets lost in email threads, submitted late, or never collected at all. This portal solves that.

1
Admins Assign, Departments Deliver
Each ranking question is routed to the right department with a deadline. No guesswork, no duplicate effort.
2
One Audited Record
Every submission is logged with timestamps and status — accepted, queried, or returned for revision. Nothing disappears.
3
Five Rankings, One Workflow
QS WUR, QS Sustainability, THE WUR, THE Impact, and UI GreenMetric are all managed here, each with its own cycle and indicator set.
QS · World University Rankings

QS World University Rankings

Published June Since 2004 1,500+ Universities

The world's most widely consulted university ranking. Evaluates institutions across 9 indicators combining global reputation surveys, Elsevier Scopus citation data, and internationalisation metrics.

  • Academic Reputation — 30% · Global survey of 140,000+ academics
  • Citations per Faculty — 20% · Research impact via Elsevier Scopus
  • Employer Reputation — 15% · Survey of 100,000+ employers worldwide
  • Faculty / Student Ratio — 10% · Teaching capacity proxy
  • International Faculty & Students — 10% · Diversity and openness signal
  • Sustainability — 5% · ESG performance score
  • Employment Outcomes — 5% · Graduate employability index
  • International Research Network — 5% · Breadth of global research partnerships
  • Human Resources — faculty headcount and ratios
  • Registrar — student enrolment and demographic data
  • Research & Graduate Studies — citations and publications
  • International Affairs — international faculty and student figures
  • Career Services — graduate employment outcome data
Focus on verifiable, publicly traceable evidence
Scopus-indexed publications, documented employer partnerships with measurable outcomes, official enrolment statistics, and joint research projects with named international partners all carry direct weight in this ranking.

QS · Sustainability Rankings

QS Sustainability Rankings

Published November Since 2023 700+ Universities

The first major ranking dedicated entirely to ESG impact. Launched in 2023, it evaluates universities across 8 categories. UoB's most data-intensive submission — 35+ question groups and 100+ sub-indicators covering environmental, social, and governance performance.

  • Environmental Impact — carbon footprint, green infrastructure
  • Social Impact — community outreach, inclusion initiatives
  • Governance — ethics policies, institutional transparency
  • Knowledge Exchange — research-to-society transfer
  • Health & Wellbeing — campus wellness programmes
  • Equality — gender equity and diversity metrics
  • Education — sustainability embedded in curriculum
  • Employability & Outcomes — green job placements
  • Sustainability Office — environmental metrics and monitoring
  • Human Resources — diversity, equality, and wellbeing data
  • Finance — ESG expenditure and sustainability budget
  • Quality Assurance — governance documentation and policies
  • Student Affairs — social outreach evidence and impact
ESG evidence must be specific and permanent
Published sustainability reports, policy documents on the UoB website, measurable outcomes (kWh reduced / tonnes diverted / people reached), and named community partners with executed projects — not just signed agreements.
THE · World University Rankings

THE World University Rankings

Published October Since 2010 2,000+ Universities

Times Higher Education's flagship ranking evaluates research-intensive universities across 5 pillars and 18 indicators. Research quality is measured using Field-Weighted Citation Impact (FWCI) from Elsevier Scopus — one of the most rigorous citation methodologies in global rankings.

  • Research Quality — 30% · FWCI, research strength and excellence
  • Teaching — 29.5% · Reputation survey, staff/student ratio, PhDs awarded
  • Research Environment — 29% · Research income, productivity, and reputation
  • International Outlook — 7.5% · International staff, students, and co-authorship
  • Industry Income — 4% · Knowledge transfer and commercial research income
  • Research & Graduate Studies — research income, publications, PhDs awarded
  • Human Resources — staff headcount and international faculty ratio
  • Finance — research income documentation by source
  • International Affairs — international co-authorship and student data
  • Industry Partnerships — technology transfer and contract research records
Research data is the backbone here
Scopus-indexed publications with FWCI above field average, documented research grant income with source, named industry partners with signed contracts and delivered outputs, and PhD completion figures by faculty.

THE · Impact Rankings

THE Impact Rankings

Published April / May Since 2019 1,900+ Universities

The only global ranking measuring universities against all 17 UN SDGs. Score = SDG 17 Partnerships (22%, mandatory) + best 3 of the remaining 16 SDGs (78%). Open to any university — no minimum research requirement.

  • SDG 17 — Partnerships (22%) · Mandatory. Cross-sector collaboration, data sharing, policy engagement.
  • Best 3 remaining SDGs (78%) · UoB selects the 3 SDGs with strongest evidence to maximise score.
  • Each SDG scored on: research output + stewardship (policy/practice) + outreach (community engagement)
  • All evidence must have a permanent, publicly accessible URL
  • ✓ External community impact with measurable, numbered outcomes
  • ✓ Published policy or official institutional strategy document
  • ✓ Executed partnership with named partner and delivered results
  • ✗ Internal activity with no community-facing impact
  • ✗ MoU signed but not yet implemented with results
  • ✗ News item without numbers or traceable evidence link
Structure every entry like this:
Community problem → UoB's role → Target group → Number of participants / beneficiaries → Executing partner → Measurable outcome → SDG reference → Permanent URL
UI GreenMetric · Campus Sustainability

UI GreenMetric World University Rankings

Published December Since 2010 1,477+ Universities · 95 Countries

The world's first campus sustainability ranking by Universitas Indonesia. Evaluates physical campus operations across 6 categories and 61 indicators. Everything must be measurable and campus-based. 2025 theme: "Doing SDGs in Higher Education."

  • Setting & Infrastructure — 15% · Green space, open land, forest area on campus
  • Energy & Climate Change — 21% · Renewable energy use, smart buildings, GHG emissions
  • Waste — 18% · Waste reduction policy, recycling programmes, sewage treatment
  • Water — 10% · Conservation, recycling, consumption monitoring
  • Transportation — 18% · Shuttle buses, bicycle lanes, EV infrastructure, car-free zones
  • Education & Research — 18% · Sustainability courses, campus budget, student organisations
  • ✓ Actual implemented campus project with a clear implementation date
  • ✓ Quantified consumption or reduction data — kWh, m³, tonnes
  • ✓ Monitoring reports with baseline comparisons showing progress
  • ✓ Documented sustainable infrastructure (solar panels, water recycling, EV chargers)
  • ✗ Awareness workshop or campaign without physical implementation
  • ✗ Photos only — no operational data or monitoring reports
  • Facilities Management — energy, water, waste operational data
  • Sustainability Office — policy documents and monitoring reports
  • IT & Digital Services — smart building and energy management systems
  • Transport & Campus Services — shuttle routes and EV infrastructure
  • Academic Affairs — sustainability-linked courses and research budget
